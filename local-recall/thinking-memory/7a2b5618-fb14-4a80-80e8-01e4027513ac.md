Stew is a classic comfort food - a slow-cooked dish where meat, vegetables, and aromatics simmer together in liquid until everything becomes tender and the flavors meld.

**Common types:**
- **Beef stew** - chunks of beef, potatoes, carrots, onions, often with red wine or beer
- **Irish stew** - lamb or mutton with potatoes and onions
- **Goulash** - Hungarian paprika-heavy beef stew
- **Chicken stew** - lighter, often with cream or white wine
- **Brunswick stew** - Southern US, traditionally with game meat and lima beans
- **Bouillabaisse** - French fish stew from Marseille

**What makes a good stew:**
- Low and slow cooking (braising tenderizes tougher cuts)
- Browning the meat first for depth of flavor
- Building layers - aromatics, then liquids, then time
- The right balance of liquid - not too soupy, not too dry
- Letting it rest (often better the next day)

**Best cuts for stew:**
Tougher, fattier cuts work best - chuck, brisket, shoulder, shank. The collagen breaks down into gelatin, creating that rich, silky texture.
